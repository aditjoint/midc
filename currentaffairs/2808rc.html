ERP Cost Related FAQs

How much does an ERP system cost? â–¼

ERP costs vary widely depending on the type of ERP (cloud vs. on-premise), number of users, modules required, and customization level. Small businesses may start from a few lakhs, while large enterprise-scale implementations can run into several crores.

What are the main components of ERP cost? â–¼

Software license/subscription, implementation & customization, hardware/infrastructure (for on-premise), training & change management, annual maintenance & support, integrations, and future upgrades.

Is ERP a one-time cost or recurring? â–¼

Both. One-time costs include implementation and setup; recurring costs include subscriptions, AMC, support, hosting, and updates.

Which is cheaper: Cloud ERP or On-Premise ERP? â–¼

Cloud ERP usually has lower upfront costs with subscription pricing and bundled hosting/updates. On-premise requires higher upfront investment, but results much lower recurring fees.

How do ERP vendors price their solutions? â–¼

Based on number of users/license slabs, modules selected, transaction volume/data size (in SaaS) and customization complexity. However, when deployed as an on-premise installation for a long-term scope (more than 3 years), ERP often becomes much more cost-optimized compared to SaaS subscriptions.

Can ERP costs be reduced for small businesses? â–¼

Yes: choose modular ERP (pay only for required modules), prefer cloud subscription, limit customization initially, and adopt phased rollout.

Are there hidden costs in ERP? â–¼

Possible hidden costs: extra customization, legacy system integration, additional training, extensive data migration, and downtime during implementation.

How long before ERP shows ROI? â–¼

Typically within 1â€“3 years depending on implementation scale, process improvements, and user adoption.

What is the cost of ERP maintenance? â–¼

On-premise: 20â€“22% of license cost annually for mid-scale setup AMC. Cloud/SaaS: maintenance and upgrades are generally included in subscription fee building high cost roll-up.

Is open-source ERP really free? What is major drawback if this is opted ? â–¼

Open-source removes license fees but you still incur more costs for hosting, customization, training, support, and upgrades. Here an implemnting agency can trick further on cost.
